## Study Tips & Strategies

### **1. Combine Subjects for Better Understanding**
- Use **Data Science tools** to analyze **economic data** or financial trends.  
- Apply **mathematical formulas** to solve real-world problems in Economics and Physics.  
- Explore **historical trends** and **geopolitical factors** when studying Economics or International Law.

### **2. Create Summary Notes & Cheat Sheets**
- Write down **key concepts, formulas, and laws** in one place.  
- Highlight **important terms** using colors or symbols.  
- Use **tables and charts** for quick reference.

### **3. Practice & Review Regularly**
- Solve **problems and exercises** for Mathematics, Data Science, and Economics.  
- Revisit **important cases and legal principles** for Law subjects.  
- Test your understanding by **explaining concepts in your own words**.

### **4. Explore Online Resources**
- Watch **tutorial videos** for complex topics (Khan Academy, Coursera).  
- Read **research papers and articles** for advanced understanding (ResearchGate, HeinOnline).  
- Use **interactive websites** like Duolingo for language learning or Kaggle for data science projects.

### **5. Use Visualizations**
- Create **graphs, charts, and dashboards** to understand data trends.  
- Map out **historical events, political boundaries, or legal systems** visually.  
- Visualization makes memorization easier and learning faster.

### **6. Organize Study Sessions**
- Use **Pomodoro technique**: 25â€“30 minutes of focused study, 5-minute breaks.  
- Focus on **one subject at a time**, but mix topics weekly for better retention.  
- Track your **progress and revisit difficult topics** regularly.
